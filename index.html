About Me

Welcome to my website! I am a research scholar at IIT Bombay, specializing in medium voltage multilevel converters for motor drive applications, I am passionate about advancing the field of power electronics and optimizing motor drive systems. My focus lies in the analysis, design, and simulation study of medium-voltage converters and the development of experiments for their verification.

I am enthusiastic about motor controllers, Electric Vehicles, real-time control implementation, and grid integration, traction drive systems, hardware development of power electronic systems, and renewable energy systems.

Research Experince

PhD Thesis: Investigation of Optimal Power Electronic Converter for Medium Voltage (MV) Drive Applications

  1. Field-Oriented Control (FOC) of Induction Motor
    • Analysis, design, and simulation of closed-loop FOC control of Induction Motor.
    • Design of signal conditioning circuits for sensor signals such as voltage, current, and speed, and a voltage level shifting circuit. Calibrating the motor speed using eQEP module in C2000 micro-controller.
    • Experimental implementation of closed-loop FOC for 5 kW induction motor.
  2. Full-Bridge Submodule-based Modular Multilevel Converter (FB-MMC) for MV Drive Applications
    • Sizing of submodule capacitor of MMC suitable for various operating points.
    • Comparative analysis of capacitive energy storage of various multilevel converter topologies for MV drive applications.
    • Challenge: Huge submodule capacitor voltage ripple during low-speed operation.
    • Analysis, modelling, and simulation of FB-MMC-based drive.
    • Proposed a control scheme for FB-MMC-based drive to minimize the capacitor voltage ripple within the acceptable limits for a wide speed range.
    • Development of a laboratory prototype of the FB-MMC drive to validate the proposed control strategy.
  3. Parallel Hybrid Converter (PHC) based Drive
    • PHC-based drive requires less capacitive energy storage, hence less weight and cost
    • Challenge: Strong coupling between the dc-link voltage and motor-side voltage.
    • Proposed a new transformerless PHC-based drive configuration and control strategy. This features low capacitive energy storage, cost, and volume.
    • Development of an Experimental prototype of the proposed transformerless PHC drive.
    • Full-Duplex Serial-Peripheral Interface (SPI) protocol has been used for communication between two micro-controllers (TMS320F28335).
  4. Active Front-End (AFE) Rectifier using Full-Bridge based Modular Multilevel Converter (FB-MMC)
    • Analysis, design, and simulation of AFE rectifier.
    • Real-time vector control implementation of Power Factor Correction (PFC) and active power control.
    • Implementation of three-phase Phased Locked Loop (PLL).
    • Realization of variable dc-link voltage.

MTech Thesis: Modelling and Simulation of Doubly Fed Induction Generator (DFIG)-based Standalone Wind Energy System with Battery Backup

    • Mathematical modelling of standalone DFIG (two back-to-back connected IGBT-based PWM converter) system.
    • Design of a closed-loop control system for Variable Speed Constant Frequency (VSCF) operation.
    • Proposed a standalone DFIG with a single converter and Battery Energy Storage.
    • Analysis, modelling, and control development of the Proposed DFIG to achieve VSCF operation.
